Prizes & Elimination

Prize Cards and Elimination

Why do Prize cards work as "life" instead of like in the official game?

In the official 1v1 game, you take Prize cards when you Knock Out your opponent's Pokémon. In a battle royale, that would encourage kill stealing: sniping a weakened Pokémon that another player worked to damage, just to grab the reward. In Totem Format nobody profits directly from a Knock Out — the defeated player takes their own Prize cards into their hand (a small comeback boost) and simply gets closer to elimination.

Who takes Prize cards when a Pokémon is Knocked Out by Poison or another effect?

The same as always: the controller of the Knocked Out Pokémon takes Prize cards from their own pile, no matter what caused the Knock Out (attack, Ability, Special Condition, or self-damage).

How do cards that compare Prize counts work, like "if you have more Prize cards remaining than your opponent"?

"Your opponent" in multiplayer always means one opponent: the one you are targeting with the attack, or one you choose when the effect resolves. You never compare against the whole table. Keep in mind that in this format the player who is winning is the one with more Prize cards left — so these cards work best against the table leader. That is intentional: it gives the losing players tools to gang up on whoever is ahead.

Is Iono good or bad for me?

Iono makes each player draw cards equal to their own remaining Prize cards. Players who have taken a beating have fewer Prizes left, so they draw fewer cards. Unlike the official game, Iono punishes the players who are already behind — time it carefully, and expect it to make you no friends.

What happens if I must take more Prize cards than I have left?

You take all your remaining Prize cards and you are eliminated, exactly as if you had taken your last one.

Can I run out of cards in my deck?

Yes. If you cannot draw a card at the beginning of your turn because your deck is empty, you are eliminated — unless a card effect says otherwise.

The Totem

Totem Pokémon

Can my Totem attack the same turn it enters play?

Yes. The Totem enters play as it is — it does not evolve, so Evolution Sickness does not apply. If it has the Energy its attack requires, it can attack immediately.

Can the Totem be evolved or devolved?

No, never. The Totem has no pre-Evolutions and cannot be evolved into anything else. Cards that devolve Pokémon have no effect on it.

What happens to my Totem when it is Knocked Out?

You take your own Prize cards according to its Rule Box, then you choose: return it to the Totem Zone (the first return is free; each later return permanently increases its Invocation Cost by 1), or send it to the discard pile (no cost increase, but it is stuck there until a card effect moves it — and then you may redirect it to the Totem Zone).

An effect wants to send my Totem to the Lost Zone / my deck / my hand. What happens?

You choose: it goes where the effect says, or it goes to the Totem Zone instead — a bit like Commander. Remember that the Totem can only enter play by invocation from the Totem Zone.

Can I put Special Energy on my Totem in the Totem Zone?

Yes, but while in the Totem Zone it counts as 1 Colorless Energy toward the Invocation Cost and is completely inert. Effects that trigger "when you attach this card" never resolve, because the attachment happened outside of play. Once the Totem enters play, the Energy works normally again — for example, Double Turbo Energy provides 2 Colorless from that moment on.

Can opponents interact with my Totem or its Energy while it is in the Totem Zone?

No. While in the Totem Zone, the Totem is not in play: it cannot be targeted, damaged, or affected by anything, and its attached Energy cannot be discarded or moved by card effects.

What Stage does my VMAX, VSTAR, BREAK, or Mega Totem use on the cost table?

VMAX and VSTAR count as Stage 1 (they evolve from a Basic Pokémon V). BREAK Pokémon count as one Stage above the Pokémon they evolve from — a BREAK of a Stage 2 uses the Stage 3 column. Mega Evolution and Primal Reversion Pokémon always use the Stage 3 column.

Can my Totem be a V-UNION or a LEGEND card?

Not for now. V-UNION and LEGEND cards are not legal in the format (neither as Totem nor in the deck). Specific rules for them may come in a future revision.

Deck Building

Deck Building

Which cards are legal?

Every Pokémon TCG card ever printed, from the original Base Set to the newest expansion, except the cards on the Banlist (and V-UNION / LEGEND cards, which are on hold).

It's a singleton format — can I play several ACE SPEC cards, since each is a different card?

No. Official global limits still apply: maximum 1 ACE SPEC in total and maximum 1 Radiant Pokémon in total per deck. Your Totem counts toward these limits: if your Totem is a Radiant Pokémon, you cannot include another Radiant in your 99.

My Pokémon's attack costs include Colorless symbols. Does that break my type identity?

No. Colorless symbols in attack costs are generic — they can be paid with any Energy and never count as a "required type". Only named Energy types (Fire, Water, Psychic…) count for identity. The Colorless restriction for Retreat Cost 0 Totems only affects Colorless-type Pokémon and Special Energy that provides Colorless.

How do dual-type Pokémon work with type identity?

If your Totem is dual-type, both of its types are part of your identity. If a dual-type Pokémon is in your deck, both of its types must be allowed by your Totem's identity.

Do opponents draw extra cards when I mulligan?

No. Mulligans are free for everyone (reveal your hand, shuffle it back, draw 7). With 100-card singleton decks, mulligans are more common than in the official game, so the official compensation draw is removed.

Multiplayer & Timing

Multiplayer and Timing

A card says "your opponent". Who is that in a 4-player game?

One opponent, chosen by the player resolving the effect (or the opponent already being targeted, for attacks). Cards that say "each player" or "all players" affect the whole table. See Rules Section 9 for the full scope rules.

When exactly can I play my 1 off-turn Item?

The exact timing windows (and what an off-turn Item can respond to) are still being playtested and will be defined in a future revision. Until then, we recommend this house rule: play off-turn Items between turns (after a player's Pokémon Checkup, before the next player draws), and never in response to an attack that has already been declared. Agree on the details as a table before the game.

Can I attack any player?

Yes. On your turn you declare an attack against one opponent of your choice. There is no forced rotation — politics, threat assessment, and deals decide who gets hit.

Are deals binding?

No. Negotiation is encouraged, betrayal is legal.

What happens to an eliminated player's cards?

All of their in-play cards (including their Stadium, if it was theirs) are discarded without triggering any "when discarded" effects, and the player is removed from the game entirely — no effect considers them anymore.
