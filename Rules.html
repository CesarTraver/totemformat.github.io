Totem Format Rules – Version 1.3

Full rule set for the Pokémon TCG Totem Format. This page explains the Totem Pokémon mechanics, Prize interactions, and multiplayer policies.

1. Overview

This format adapts Pokémon TCG for multiplayer play (3–X players), keeping Prize Cards compatible. Matches use rotating objectives, negotiation, and thematic singleton decks centered on a Totem Pokémon.

2. Players

3–X players (recommended 3–6). All versus all format.

3. Setup

  • Deck: 100 cards, singleton rules.
  • Totem Pokémon placed in top-right area.
  • 6 Prize cards face down.
  • Standard Pokémon TCG opening hand.
  • Mulligans free until a Basic Pokémon is in hand (reveal hand when mulliganing).

4. Deck Rules

  • Exactly 100 cards (99 + 1 Totem Pokémon).
  • No duplicates except Basic Energy.
  • Pokémon can repeat only if from different expansions and with different stats, attacks, or abilities.
  • No cards with rule boxes allowed in the deck.
  • All Pokémon must be exclusively of Totem Pokémon defined types.
  • Attacks must not require Energies outside Totem Pokémon types.

5. Totem Pokémon

Definition: Totem is a Pokémon with rule box, starts in top-right corner outside the play area. Not active initially.

Type Identity:

  1. Totem base type always allowed.
  2. Totem attack energy types count as base (except Colorless).
  3. If Totem has retreat cost > 0, Colorless Energy allowed.
  4. If Totem has 0 retreat cost, Colorless not allowed.
  5. If Totem attack uses Colorless but retreat is 0: Colorless ignored, cannot include Colorless Pokémon or special Energy giving Colorless.

Invocation Cost:

  • Basic: 2 energies
  • Stage 1: 3 energies
  • Stage 2: 4 energies

During your turn, assign an energy to Totem instead of Pokémon; counts as your turn energy.

Entering Play: Totem must enter automatically when enough energy and space exists. Can go to bench or active slot, removing current active for free. Cannot add last energy if Totem would auto-enter but space is blocked.

Defeat: First defeat no cost increase. Subsequent defeats increase cost permanently by 1, cannot be reduced by effects.

6. Prize Cards

Each player starts with 6 Prize cards.

When a Pokémon is defeated:

  • Normal Pokémon: 1 Prize
  • Totem: depends on rule box

7. Prize Interactions

Effects that reference prizes swap the active player's prizes with the targeted opponent's prizes for resolution purposes only.

8. Rounds & Objectives

Matches divided into full rounds. Objectives assigned with a dX die where X = active players. Targets may repeat across rounds.

9. Mandatory Attacks

You must attack your assigned target. If not possible, discard 7 cards from your hand.

10. Items

Items can be played outside your turn, maximum 1 per player per round. Effects may target an opponent, paying all costs yourself.

11. Stadiums

Only one Stadium in play. All other Stadium rules follow standard TCG format.

12. Player Elimination

Players eliminated when out of Prize cards. Upon elimination, they are removed from objectives and all in-play cards are discarded. Discarded cards do not trigger "when discarded" effects.

13. Victory Condition

Last player standing wins.
